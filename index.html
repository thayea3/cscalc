    A lighting Design Guideline was developed through Underwriters Laboratories (UL) to support circadian entrainment for day-active/night-inactive individuals. This is known as UL DG 24480 (2019).1

    A Circadian Stimulus (CS) value of 0.3 for 2 hours, ideally in the morning, is recommended for daytime light exposures. This daytime light exposure is defined as a circadian-effective dose of light, quantified in terms of both the amount of circadian-effective light (CLA) and the duration (hours) of light exposure. Functionally, the recommended circadian-effective light dose (CSd) from UL DG 24480 is CSd= 0.43. A CSd value can be obtained by either changing the amount of circadian-effective light (CLA) or the duration (hours) of light exposure. A reciprocal relationship between amount and duration has been empirically demonstrated within the duration limits of 0.5 to 3 hours.2 So, for example, a 2 hour exposure to 500 CLA is equivalent to a 1 hour exposure of 1000 CLA.

    This latest version of the CS calculator provides values of CLA, as it always has, for any spectral power distribution and photopic illuminance, but, based on the reported value of CLA, now reports the duration of light exposure needed to reach the UL DG 24480 circadian-effective light dose of CSd = 0.43
